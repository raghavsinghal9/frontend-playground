# 1. DSA — company-tagged + daily warm-up
- Check each target company's tagged questions (LeetCode company filter / interview-experience posts) and do those patterns.
- Keep a **daily warm-up** of 1–2 problems so you stay sharp through the interview period — never go cold.
- You're maintaining, not cramming: trust the patterns you've built over 11 weeks.

# 2. JavaScript — rapid revision
Quick-fire across everything: closures, `this`/`call`/`apply`/`bind`, prototypes/`new`, event loop ordering, Promises, array polyfills, event delegation, memory leaks. Then **redo your shaky polyfills** from a blank file — `debounce`, `throttle`, `Promise.all`, `deepClone`, `myBind`, Event Emitter. If you can write all of those cold, your JS coding round is covered.

# 3. Machine coding — full mock round
Simulate the real thing twice this week:
- **45 min, blank editor, no autocomplete, narrate continuously.**
- Pick a component you *haven't* rebuilt recently (so it's a real test).
- Checklist: clarify → break down → working skeleton → edge cases (empty/loading/error, keyboard, a11y) → refactor.
- The signal interviewers want: you build something that **works**, you **talk through** trade-offs, and you **catch your own bugs**.

# 4. System design — full mock round
- **40 minutes, drive the whole thing with RADIO**, ideally on Excalidraw (the tool many remote interviews use — get familiar with it).
- Redo two designs out loud: one component (autocomplete or carousel) and one application (news feed, e-commerce, or chat).
- Remember the time split: spend most of it on **Optimizations & deep dive**, and **lead with the axis that defines the product** (SEO for e-commerce, real-time for chat, a11y/API for a design system).
- Drive from a vague prompt — reduce ambiguity yourself; don't wait to be guided.

# 5. Behavioral — finalize your 6 STAR stories
Polish these to ~2 minutes each, result-focused (Situation → Task → Action → Result):
1. **Ownership at scale** — owning the frontend for HR domains used across a large enterprise client's entire workforce.
2. **Technical leadership** — leading the Angular 18→20 migration.
3. **Complex system** — the configurable leave policy engine with automated routing.
4. **Conflict / disagreement** — a time you pushed back on an approach.
5. **Mentoring / collaboration** — bringing teammates up on Angular patterns.
6. **Failure / learning** — a bug or miss and what you changed.

Practice them out loud until they're natural, not memorized-sounding.

# 6. Applying — full push (the part that actually lands offers)
You should already be applying from earlier weeks; now go full-throttle on the channels we set up:
- **Referrals first** — they bypass resume screens and are your highest-leverage path, especially for SDE-2. Reach out to your network and 2nd-degree connections at target companies.
- **Apply Angular-first** — less competition and high GCC demand; you're a specialist there.
- **Target the 2–4 year experience band**; aim SDE-2, accept a well-paid SDE-1.
- **Channels:** LinkedIn + Naukri primary; Instahyre + Cutshort secondary. Keep profiles active (small edits surface you to recruiters).
- **GCCs + product startups** in Bengaluru.
- Treat early interviews as **practice** — every loop sharpens you.

# 7. Negotiation — quick reminders for when offers come
- **Expected ~₹17 LPA** (target band 14–20; don't anchor below ~15).
- **Confirm the level** (SDE-1 vs SDE-2) early — it drives the band.
- **Don't reveal your current salary too early**; let them put a number down first where possible.
- **Use competing offers** as leverage if you have them; interview at a few places so timelines overlap.
- It's a conversation, not a confrontation — be warm, be specific, know your floor.

# 8. Interview-day tactics
- Coding: plain editor, **narrate**, clarify before coding, test your own output, fix bugs calmly.
- System design: **clarify scope first**, then **drive** with RADIO; manage your time; think out loud.
- If you blank: state your approach, start simple, iterate — interviewers reward process.
- It's fine to say "I'd reach for X here, and the trade-off is Y" — that's senior thinking.
